The Deer and the Grapes: Long ago, in a distant forest, there lived a deer that roamed the woods. One day, it stumbled upon a vineyard filled with delicious grapes hanging from the vines, looking incredibly tempting. The aroma of the grapes reached the deer's nostrils, sparking its curiosity. However, the grapes were hanging high up, out of the deer's reach. It tried jumping and stretching its neck, but all its efforts were in vain. The deer felt frustrated and began to mock the grapes, claiming they must be sour and not worth the trouble. Just as the deer was about to leave, a wise old fox appeared. The old fox noticed the deer's embarrassment and disappointment, so it approached the deer. "Friend," the old fox said, "why do you mock those grapes? They might truly be delicious, only you haven't tried hard enough." The deer was puzzled, but it decided to heed the old fox's advice. Closing its eyes, the deer focused its energy and began to jump repeatedly, attempting to reach the grapes. Though still challenging, the deer stopped mocking the grapes and concentrated diligently. Finally, after several attempts, the deer managed to grasp a bunch of grapes. As it savored the sweet taste, it felt elated. It had learned a valuable lesson ‚Äì that true rewards come from putting in effort and exercising patience.
